Real-World Architecture Usage

2D CAD (AutoCAD, CorelDRAW)

Both laptops perform excellent. No noticeable difference.

3D Modelling (SketchUp, Rhino, Revit)

Victus is slightly smoother with very heavy scenes.
Vivobook handles student-level projects extremely well.

Real-Time Rendering (Twinmotion, Enscape)

Victus is about 15–20% faster.
But the Vivobook still runs these smoothly for academic use.

Travel + College Reality

She travels 2 hours.
She carries heavy material already.
She does not render full projects on a bus.

Vivobook makes more practical sense.

Why Vivobook is the Smarter Choice

  • ✔️ Lighter by ~400 grams (huge difference daily)
  • ✔️ Better battery efficiency during moderate work
  • ✔️ OLED screen improves design review & presentation
  • ✔️ Still powerful enough for all academic architecture workloads
  • ✔️ More balanced machine overall

The Honest Truth

Yes, the RTX 5050 is stronger.

But the performance gap (~20%) does not outweigh:

  • Extra weight
  • Shorter heavy-use battery
  • Gaming-oriented bulk
For an architecture student, balance > raw GPU power.

Final Recommendation

Choose the ASUS Vivobook Pro 16.

It delivers the best combination of performance, portability, battery, and display quality for architecture studies.
